# mdpeek — Product context

## Product purpose
A featherlight Markdown viewer/editor for Windows. The soul job is reading and
editing `.md`/`.txt` files fast, with zero ceremony. Not an IDE, not a
notebook, not a knowledge base. A viewer that respects the reader.

## Register
Product. Design serves the task. The tool should disappear into reading and
writing. Earned familiarity over surprise.

## Users
- Developers reading READMEs, CHANGELOGs, and docs locally.
- Writers and note-takers who live in plain text.
- People who double-click a `.md` file and want it to open instantly, in
  something lighter than VS Code.

## Brand tone
Quiet, precise, fast. Not playful, not corporate. Closer to iA Writer than to
Notion. The content is the focus; the chrome stays out of the way.

## Anti-references
- Notion: too much UI, too many affordances, too heavy.
- Typora: beautiful but the seamless WYSIWYG hides the markdown, which many
  users don't want.
- VS Code: too much chrome for "just read a markdown file."

## Strategic principles
1. Content first. The rendered document owns the screen.
2. Chrome recedes. Toolbar, tabs, sidebar are thin and quiet.
3. Speed is a feature. If it's not instant, it's broken.
4. Familiar, not novel. Standard reading affordances; no invented patterns.

## Design notes
- Dual theme: light (default) and dark. Both must be excellent.
- System font stack (already in place). No web fonts.
- Restrained color: tinted neutrals + one accent (GitHub-style blue) for
  active/selection state only.
- Generous reading measure; comfortable line height for long-form docs.
